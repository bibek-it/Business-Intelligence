2 The Control Panel: How to Filter Your Data

These filters are the most important controls. They change the entire dashboard to show you exactly what you need to see. Mastering these gives you complete control over your data view.

Quick Time Filters & Report Period

These are shortcuts for common date ranges. The Report Period is your custom date range selector.

All KPIs and charts that depend on a date will use this range.

Interview Date Context (Crucial Filter)

This filter changes the definition of an 'interview' for the entire dashboard. It answers the question: 'When do we count an interview?'

Context Filter - This determines how interviews are counted across the entire dashboard
  • By Interview Start Date (Default): Counts an interview when it happened. Use this to analyze performance.
  • By Interview Created Date: Counts an interview when it was scheduled. Use this to measure team productivity.

3 The KPI Cards: Your Key Metrics & Their Logic

These are the main numbers at the top. Here is exactly how each one is calculated based on your selected Report Period. Click 'View Logic' to see the detailed calculation.

Total Submissions

How it's Calculated:

We count every submission where the Submission Created Date falls within your selected Report Period.

Total Interviews

How it's Calculated:

We count every interview based on the Interview Date Context filter you selected. By default, it counts all interviews where the Interview Start Date is within your selected Report Period.

Hired

How it's Calculated:

We count all submissions where the Hired Date is not blank AND the status is Hired or Hired External. The Hired Date must fall within your selected Report Period.

4 The Recruitment Conversion Funnel

This shows the percentage of candidates who move from one key stage to the next. Its calculation depends on a critical choice.

How it's Calculated:

The funnel's data source can be switched. It measures candidates who entered the funnel based on either:

Funnel Data Source - This determines how candidates are included in the conversion funnel
  • By Initial Interview Date: The funnel includes all candidates who had their first interview within the selected period. This shows your conversion efficiency for the people you actually spoke to.
  • By Submission Created Date: The funnel includes all candidates who applied within the selected period. This shows your end-to-end conversion efficiency starting from the very first application.

5 The Distribution Charts: Your Interactive Breakdowns

These charts let you segment your data and act as powerful filters for the entire dashboard view.

Submission Status & Distribution

Based on all submissions with a Submission Created Date within your selected Report Period.

Click on any bar to filter the entire dashboard to show only data for candidates with that status.

Interview Distributions (Level, Mode, Status, Tier)

These charts are based on the Interview Date Context filter. By default, they count all interviews where the Interview Start Date is within your selected Report Period.

Click on any segment to filter the entire dashboard to see only candidates from that specific interview group.

6 The Data Tables: Your Detailed Records

The Submission Details and Interview Details tables at the bottom show the individual, line-by-line data. These tables are the final output of all your filtering. Every selection you make in the Control Panel, every KPI you consider, and every chart segment you click will refine the data shown in these tables. They are your source for drilling down to the exact candidate or interview you need to investigate.
